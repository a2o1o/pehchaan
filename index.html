How the Idea Came To Be

Pehchaan began with small one-on-one calls and discussions with girls from underserved communities. These conversations revealed their aspirations and struggles with confidence, language comfort, and storytelling. Inspired by these insights, the program was designed to provide structured scaffolds and a safe space where expression comes before correction.

Proof of Continuity

Pehchaan is a journey, not a one-off idea. Here’s how it has grown step by step:

2024 – Background research with girls in South Delhi to understand challenges and needs.
Early 2025 – Program design, lesson planning, and NGO partnerships to shape the sessions.
July 2025 – Batch 1 pilot with 4 girls from Saijaya Youth Trust in Kalkaji, 3 sessions covering identity, storytelling, and final speech.
Aug 2025 – Impact Report published, with before/after recordings, poll surveys, and testimonials. Featured in Saijaya Trust’s blog.

Impact Narrative – Batch 1

The process began with short calls and discussions, then a structured lesson plan was developed. Sessions guided participants through introductions, personal stories, body language, and eye contact practice. The pilot with 4 girls from Saijaya Youth Trust resulted in measurable growth:

  • Greater confidence and less stage fright
  • Improved posture, eye contact, and tone
  • Joy in being listened to and forming sisterhood
  • Future readiness for classrooms and interviews

What Makes Pehchaan Different

  • Started with deep listening: short calls and discussions built the foundation before planning lessons.
  • Confidence-first, not grammar-first: we focus on expression over correction.
  • Story scaffolds, not random topics: prompts like “Who I Am” and “A Moment That Shaped Me” anchor real storytelling.
  • Measured impact: progress tracked with before/after videos, polls, and surveys.
  • Peer-to-peer mentorship: relatable coaching from students close in age.
  • Trusted partners: Collaboration with Saijaya Youth Trust, whose blog featured our pilot success.

Future Direction

Pehchaan is alive and growing. Here’s where we go next:

  • Batch 2: Expanding to 8–12 girls with peer mentors and alumni buddies.
  • Showcase Platform: Final speeches will be recorded and shared with families and partners.
  • Mentorship Chain: Participants from Batch 1 will guide new cohorts as peer mentors.
